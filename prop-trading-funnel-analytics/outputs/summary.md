# Executive Summary

- The synthetic case covers **18,000 registrations**, **2,232 purchasing users**, and **2,676 challenge purchases**.
- **580 challenges** reach funded status and **302 challenges** record an approved payout.
- Gross fee revenue is contrasted against **$674,504** of payout exposure and **$592,472** of approved payouts.
- The strongest acquisition channel on the gross profit proxy is **paid_social**, while **community** looks weakest once payout exposure is netted against fee revenue.
- The most balanced challenge type is **aggressive**, while **swing** carries the largest payout exposure footprint.
- The top funded exposure segment is **community | advanced**, with **$158,865** of payout exposure and a **65.5%** payout rate.
- A misleading top-of-funnel segment is **direct | advanced**, which converts well into purchases but falls behind on gross profit proxy once payout exposure is included.

## Recommendations

- Optimize media and partner spend on downstream quality, not registrations or first purchase alone.
- Keep low-cost trials as an acquisition product, but do not treat their top-of-funnel conversion as evidence of trader quality.
- Review high-exposure funded segments with a combined lens: funded rate, payout rate, and payout exposure per funded challenge.
- Use behavior-based monitoring earlier in the lifecycle. Higher risk per trade, lower win rate, and more rule violations are visible before funded status.
